5.102. simplified-reader-bookmarks

5.102.1. Issues

5.102.1.1. Grossly Overengineered Bookmark Policy

1
The bookmark policy class is overengineered and unnecessarily complicated. The historical reason for this was that the mechanism for storing bookmarks, parsing bookmarks, and sending and receiving bookmarks to/from the server used to be deeply intertwined and contained within a single method (!) in the R1 reader activity. As part of refactoring, these separate concerns were broken up into their own implementation classes and moved into this module. At the time, due to the complexity of the code, we were unsure as to exactly when and why the application would choose to send or receive bookmarks. The policy was reverse-engineered as much as it could be, and a pure functional implementation of the policy was placed into a class with the intention of refining and expanding the policy at a later date. It turns out that the only policy the application needs is "send all bookmarks as soon as a bookmark is created" and "receive all bookmarks when a book is opened". The complexity in the current policy is entirely unwarranted.
2
Issue class: COMPLEX. Severity: Major.

1
